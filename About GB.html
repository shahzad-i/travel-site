History of Gilgit-Baltistan

Ancient Times

Gilgit-Baltistan was historically part of the Silk Road, connecting China, Central Asia, and the Indian subcontinent. Ancient inscriptions in Kharosthi, Brahmi, and Sanskrit found in the region show the influence of Buddhism, Hinduism, and Zoroastrianism. Buddhist monasteries and rock carvings (like those in Hunza and Skardu) indicate that GB was a cultural hub as early as the 1st century CE.

Liberation and Accession to Pakistan (1947)

In November 1947, after the creation of Pakistan, the local Gilgit Scouts revolted against the Dogra governor. Present Status

Gilgit-Baltistan is not yet a full constitutional province of Pakistan, but efforts are ongoing to grant it that status. It remains strategically important, especially due to the China-Pakistan Economic Corridor (CPEC) and its proximity to borders with China, India, and Afghanistan.
