Tourist Destinations

  1. Hunza Valley

    2. Hunza Valley is a magical destination where nature, history, and hospitality come together. With its dramatic landscapes, serene lakes, and ancient culture, it truly feels like a piece of paradise on Earth.

    Location

    Hunza Valley lies on the Karakoram Highway (KKH), about 100 km from Gilgit city. It is divided into three main regions: Upper Hunza (Gojal) – Includes Passu, Sost, and Khunjerab Central Hunza – Includes Karimabad and Altit Lower Hunza – Includes Nasirabad and Aliabad

    Top Attractions in Hunza

    • Baltit Fort

      • A 700-year-old fort overlooking Karimabad, restored beautifully and offering panoramic views of the valley.

    • Altit Fort

      • One of the oldest firts in Hunza, home to the royal family of Hunza, with stunning architecture and gardens.

    • Attabad Lake

      • A turquoise-blue lake formed after a landslide in 2010 — perfect for boating, jet skiing, and photography.

    • Passu Cones

      • Majestic pointed peaks that look like natural cathedrals a must-see for every visitor.

    • Khunjerab Pass

      • The highest paved international border crossing in the world, connecting Pakistan with China at 4,693 meters.

    • Hussaini Suspension Bridge

      • One of the most thrilling and adventurous rope bridges in the world.

    Best Time to Visit

    Spring (April–May): Cherry blossom season — the valley turns pink and white.

    Summer (June–September): Pleasant weather and perfect for trekking.

    Autumn (October): The trees turn golden-orange — ideal for photographers.

    Culture & People

    The people of Hunza are known for: Speaking Burushaski and Wakhi languages Living long, healthy lives (Hunza is famous for its high life expectancy) Being peaceful, educated, and incredibly hospitable

    Activities in Hunza

    Hiking and trekking (e.g., Ultar Glacier, Passu Glacier)

    Boating at Attabad Lake

    Exploring historic forts

    Trying local cuisine (Chapshuro, Hunza bread, apricot juice)

    Stargazing at night in open valleys

  2. Skardu

    3. Skardu is not just a destination — it's an experience of towering mountains, ancient forts, tranquil lakes, and warm culture. Whether you're chasing adventure or peace, Skardu welcomes you with unforgettable views and open hearts.

    Location

    • Altitude: ~2,500 meters (8,200 ft)
    • Located along the banks of the Indus River, about 8–10 hours' drive from Gilgit City or a short flight from Islamabad (weather-dependent).

    Top Tourist Attractions in Skardu

    • Shangrila Resort & Lower Kachura Lake

      • Also known as "Heaven on Earth," this picturesque lake and resort offer boat rides, cottages, and unforgettable views.

    • Upper Kachura Lake

      • A deep, crystal-clear natural lake ideal for boating, fishing, and peaceful nature walks.

    • Shigar Fort

      • A beautifully restored 400-year-old fort turned into a luxury hotel — a perfect blend of history and hospitality.

    • Skardu Fort (Kharpocho Fort)

      • An ancient fort with panoramic views of Skardu city and the surrounding valleys.

    • Deosai National Park (The Land of Giants)

      • One of the highest plateaus in the world (4,114 meters), filled with wildflowers, marmots, and yaks in summer — like a natural paradise.

    • Satpara Lake

      • A glacial lake near Skardu, ideal for boat rides and photography, with snow-covered peaks in the background.

    • Sheosar Lake

      • Located inside Deosai National Park, known for its reflective surface and peaceful environment.

    • Cold Desert (Sarfaranga)

      • One of the highest cold deserts in the world — with white sand dunes surrounded by mountains.

    • Manthoka Waterfall

      • A scenic, powerful waterfall located near Kharmang — perfect for a day trip.

    Culture & People

    Inhabitants are Balti people, known for their hospitality, rich history, and unique culture.


    Language: Balti (Tibetan origin)
    Food:

    Try Balti soup, momo (dumplings), and khurba (barley bread)

    .

    Best Time to Visit

    May to October is ideal for sightseeing, trekking, and lake tours.

    • Spring (April–May): Cherry and apricot blossoms.
    • Summer (June–August): Trekking and Deosai season.
    • Autumn (September–October): Golden trees and clear skies.
